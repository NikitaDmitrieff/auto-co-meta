# Landing Page Copy — v1 Decision Record

**Agent:** marketing-godin
**Cycle:** 5
**Date:** 2026-03-06

## Copy Direction: Developer-First

We target a specific tribe: **technical founders and developers** who want to build a product without running a company. They understand agents, they've used Claude, and they're tired of wearing every hat.

This is NOT a founder-first pitch ("grow your startup 10x"). That's too broad and too generic.

## Headline Decision

**Kept:** "Ship your AI company in a day."

Rationale: It's remarkable, specific, and tribal. It passes the "nobody else would say this" test. The word "company" (not "product") does important work — it signals 14 agents, strategy, the whole stack. "In a day" creates appropriate tension and signals low friction.

Alternatives considered and rejected:
- "Your AI company runs itself." → passive, not energizing
- "14 AI agents. One command." → feature-led, not outcome-led
- "Let the machines run the company." → too sci-fi, loses technical credibility

## Subheadline Revision

**Before:**
> "Auto-Co is an open-source framework for running a fully autonomous AI company — 14 AI agents, a 24/7 work loop, and zero human involvement in daily decisions."

**After:**
> "14 specialized AI agents — CEO, CTO, engineers, designers, marketers — running in a 24/7 loop. They debate, decide, and ship. You watch the dashboard and approve money."

Rationale: The original reads like a README. The revision speaks to what the user *does* (watches, approves money) — minimal. The tension between "they do everything" and "you only approve money" makes the product feel real and specific. "Approve money" is concrete and memorable.

## What We Kept

- Terminal code block in Hero (3-line setup is the product demo)
- "Open Source — MIT License" badge (developer trust signal)
- Feature grid structure (6 cards is right for this stage)
